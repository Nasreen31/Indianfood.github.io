POORI MASALA


Poori Masala is a South Indian dish of curried potatoes made to serve as a side with Poori or Puri, an Indian fried bread. Poori masala also known as poori curry is made with boiled potatoes, onions, tomatoes, whole spices and curry leaves


  • Prep Time: 30 Mins
  • Cook Time: 50 Mins
  • Servings: 4-5 persons

Ingredients:


To Make Poori


  • Wheat Flour - 2 Cups
  • Salt - 1 Tsp
  • Oil - 2 Tsp
  • Gradually Add Water
  • Oil For Deep Frying

    • To Make Potato Masala


  • Boiled Potato - 3 Nos
  • Oil - 1 Tbsp
  • Urad Dal - 1/2 Tsp
  • Chana Dal - 1/2 Tsp
  • Cumin Seeds - 1/2 Tsp
  • Mustard Seeds - 1/2 Tsp
  • Red Chilli - 3 Nos
  • Green Chilli - 3 Nos
  • Curry Leaves
  • Onion - 1 No. Thinly Sliced
  • Pound Ginger
  • Salt - 1 Tsp
  • Turmeric Powder - 1/2 Tsp
  • Asafoetida Powder - 1/4 Tsp
  • Water
  • Besan Flour - 2 Tsp
  • Chopped Coriander Leaves

Method:


  1. Take the wheat flour in a large bowl.

  2. Add salt and mix it all once. Then add oil and mix it all well again.

  3. Gradually add water to the dough and make into a nice dough ball.

  4. Once the dough is ready, just knead it for 5 mins.

  5. After kneading the dough, apply some oil on it and cover the bowl with a lid. rest the dough for 1 hour.

  6. After 1 hour, divide the dough into small balls and keep aside.

  7. Heat a kadai with oil and in the meanwhile, roll out the dough balls gently.

  8. Make sure the edges are slightly thinner than the middle part.

  9. Once the oil is hot enough, gently drop in the rolled out dough poori and keep flipping it gently to make sure it is fried to golden brown colour on both sides. Gently remove it from the kadai and keep it aside.

  10. Repeat the process with other dough balls.

  11. Hot pooris are ready to be served.

    For the Potato Masala:


  1. Take oil in a wide kadai.

  2. Add in urad dal and chana dal. Once they are roasted till golden brown, add in the cumin seeds and mustard seeds.

  3. Once the mustard seeds start splattering, add in the red chillies, green chillies and curry leaves.

  4. Now add in the sliced onions and saute for a while.

  5. To it, add the pounded ginger and saute for a few seconds again.

  6. Now add salt, turmeric powder and asafoetida powder to it.

  7. Then add the mashed potato to the kadai and mix well.

  8. Add generous amount of water to make sure the bhaji is going. Cover the kadai with a lid and cook the bhaji for 5-8 mins.

  9. Meanwhile, take the besan and water in a small bowl, mix well and keep it aside.
  10. Once the bhaji is a bit thick, add in the besan mixture, mix immediately and cook for 2-3 mins.

  11. After the bhaji is fully cooked without any rawness left in it, add in the chopped coriander leaves and mix well.

  12. Turn off the stove and keep the Bhaji aside.

  13. Poori Bhaji is ready to be served hot and nice along with some hot pooris.
